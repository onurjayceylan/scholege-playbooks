How to Run a Perfect 45-Minute Speaking Club

Complete Lesson Flow Playbook

Page 1: Lesson Structure Overview

The 45-Minute Framework

Our Speaking Club follows a proven structure that maximizes student engagement and language development in every 45-minute session.

Standard Breakdown:

  • 0-5 min: Warm-up & Connection
  • 5-15 min: Review & Goals
  • 15-30 min:strong> Main Activity
  • 30-40 min: Practice & Feedback
  • 40-45 min: Closing & Homework
Consistency Matters: Following this structure helps students know what to expect and builds confidence.

Page 2: Minutes 0-5 - Warm-Up

Setting the Tone

The first five minutes establish rapport, comfort, and enthusiasm for the lesson.

Warm-Up Checklist:

  • Greet student warmly and genuinely
  • Comment on something positive (appearance, progress)
  • Ask about their day/week casually
  • Share a brief personal anecdote if appropriate
  • Check student mood and energy level
  • Transition to lesson goals smoothly
Psychology Tip: Positive, warm openings increase student participation by up to 40%.
div>
div>

Page 3: Minutes 5-10 - Review

Connecting to Previous Learning

Briefly review what the student learned last class to build continuity and confidence.

Review Process:

  • Refer to notes from last lesson
  • Ask student what they remember
  • Praise them for recall (builds confidence)
  • Briefly revisit any confusing points
  • Connect to today's learning

This 5-minute review takes only 5 minutes but significantly improves retention.

div>

Page 4: Minutes 10-15 - Goal Setting

What We're Learning Today

Clearly state and explain today's learning objectives so students understand the purpose.

Goal-Setting Framework:

  • What: "Today we're focusing on..."
  • Why: "This will help you..."
  • How:strong> "We'll practice by..."
  • Success: "You'll know you succeeded when..."
Student Autonomy: Ask what the student wants to focus on if possible. Ownership increases engagement.
div>
div>

Page 5: Minutes 15-20 - Instruction

Teaching New Material

Present the new concept or skill clearly and concisely. Avoid information overload.

Teaching Best Practices:

  • Use multiple examples (at least 3)
  • Show, don't just tell
  • Use visual aids and screen share
  • Speak slowly and clearly
  • Check understanding frequently
  • Ask "Does this make sense?" not "Do you understand?"

The goal is clarity, not impressive vocabulary. Simplicity wins.

div>

Page 6: Minutes 20-25 - Demonstration

Modeling the Skill

Show the student exactly what you want them to do before they try it.

Demonstration Steps:

  • Do the activity once yourself (slowly)
  • Explain your thinking/strategy aloud
  • Point out common mistakes to avoid
  • Do it again (at normal speed)
  • Ask student to explain what they saw
Model First, Then Practice: This is critical for language learning. Never skip this step.
div>
div>

Page 7: Minutes 25-35 - Guided Practice

Your Turn (With Support)

The student practices with your guidance. Provide scaffolding and support.

Practice Guidance:

  • Start with easier version of activity
  • Provide sentence starters or prompts
  • Listen actively without interrupting
  • Take notes on errors (but don't correct immediately)
  • Give encouragement after each attempt
  • Gradually reduce support level

This is the heart of the lesson. Students learn by doing, not watching.

div>

Page 8: Minutes 35-40 - Feedback & Correction

Learning Through Feedback

Provide constructive feedback that motivates and teaches. Balance praise with correction.

Feedback Formula:

  • 1. Praise: "That was great because..."
  • 2. Clarify: "What you said was..."
  • 3. Correct:strong> "A better way to say that is..."
  • 4. Encourage: "Try saying it that way next time"
Never Shame: Always correct privately and kindly. Public correction damages confidence.
div>
div>

Page 9: Minutes 40-42 - Independent Practice

Free Speaking Practice

Allow the student to speak freely on the topic with minimal support. Monitor but don't interrupt.

Independent Practice:

  • Give a conversation prompt or scenario
  • Let the student speak for 1-2 minutes uninterrupted
  • Don't correct during speaking (write notes)
  • Show you're listening (nod, smile, make notes)
  • Record if possible for later review
div>

Page 10: Minutes 42-44 - Closing

Wrapping Up Well

A strong closing reinforces learning and motivates the student for future lessons.

Closing Process:

  • Summarize what was learned today
  • Celebrate specific achievements (be specific!)
  • Preview next lesson topic
  • Assign homework/practice task
  • Express genuine enthusiasm for next class
Homework is Essential: 15 minutes daily practice between lessons doubles learning outcomes.
div>
div>

Page 11: Minutes 44-45 - Goodbye

Ending Strong

The final seconds of the lesson create lasting impressions. Make them count.

Goodbye Ritual:

  • Confirm exact time of next class
  • Shake hands virtually or wave enthusiastically
  • Thank them for their effort
  • Say something encouraging about their progress
  • End exactly at 45 minutes (respect their time)

Students remember how the lesson ended. Make it positive!

div>

Page 12: Post-Class Documentation

After the Lesson Ends

Complete detailed notes within 2 hours while the lesson is fresh in your mind.

Documentation Checklist:

  • Student engagement level (1-10)
  • Topics covered and progress made
  • Strengths demonstrated today
  • Areas for improvement
  • Grammar/pronunciation focus for next class
  • Homework assigned and how student responded
  • Recommended next lesson topic
Pro Tip: Read your notes before the next class to personalize the lesson and show the student you care.
